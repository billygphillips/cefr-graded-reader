The back room smelled of old wood and dust. Kai set his bag down near the door and looked around. The walls were bare. The window was small and dirty. Grey morning light came through the glass.

He started near the door. He picked up a broken chair and put it in a rubbish bag. He swept dust from the corners. Then he crossed to the built-in cupboard on the far wall and pulled it open.

The shelf inside was empty except for one thing. A small video camera sat at the back, just behind the door. It was old and grey with a cracked plastic case. Kai picked it up. He knew he should put it in the rubbish bag. He could not.

He turned the camera over in his hands. On the side was a small screen. He pressed a button. The screen lit up. The battery was low, but the camera still had power.

He pressed play.

The screen showed this same room. He recognised the window and the cupboard. A family stood together near the centre — a woman, a man, and two children. They all looked frightened. Their faces were tight and pale. The woman held the children close.

The man knelt on the floor. He pushed his fingers under a floorboard near the wall and lifted it. He placed a small metal box in the gap below. Then he took a hammer and nailed the floorboard back down. He hit the nail hard — once, twice, three times. He stood up and looked straight at the camera.

The screen went black. The tape was over.

Kai sat still for a moment. Then he knelt on the floor.

He pressed both hands on the first floorboard near the wall. It did not move. He pressed the next one. Nothing. He worked slowly across the room, pressing on each board. They all felt the same. Old, dark wood, close together. Some creaked under his weight. None of them lifted.

He tried the boards near the corners. He tried the boards near the centre. His hands were grey with dust. Still nothing moved.

The light through the window grew thin. The room was getting dark. Kai could not keep searching in the dark. He picked up the camera and placed it carefully in his bag, next to his cleaning supplies. He would watch the tape again at home. He would look more carefully at the man's hands and at the exact spot on the floor.

He picked up his bag and walked to the hallway.

He stopped.

On the hallway floor, in the thin layer of dust, there was a long straight mark. It started at the foot of the stairs and went all the way to the front door. The mark was wide and smooth. Something heavy was dragged across that floor, from the stairs to the door.

Kai did not move. He did not see that mark when he arrived. He was sure of it.

He looked up at the dark staircase. The stairs disappeared into the shadow above. He listened.

The house was quiet.
