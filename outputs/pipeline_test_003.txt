He tried the lid. He pressed it. He pulled it. It did not move. The box was locked.

He turned it over. On the bottom, he saw a name in the metal. Someone cut the letters deep. Engraved. Sharp and clear. He read the name slowly. He read it again. He did not know this name. It was a stranger's name on a stranger's box.

He sat on the floor. He turned the locked box in his hands. He searched every side — every edge, every corner. The metal was smooth everywhere but the bottom. There, the engraved letters stayed sharp under his thumb.

Old. Important. Somebody's.

He forgot about the time. He forgot about the cleaning. He forgot about Hadley.

Then he heard it.

An engine. Outside in the street.
