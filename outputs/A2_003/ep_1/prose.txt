Tom carried his bucket and brush up the stairs. Each step made a soft sound under his feet. The house was old and empty. The air smelled of dust.

The bedroom at the top had old furniture. There was a bed frame, two chairs, and a long table. Light came through the dirty window in thin lines. Tom started on the floor. He moved the brush in long lines and pushed the dust to the wall.

He moved one of the chairs. Behind it was a wooden cupboard. Tom opened the door. A smell of old wood came out.

On the middle shelf was an old video camera.

Tom put his brush down. He picked up the camera. It was small and black. Dust covered the top of it. He pressed a button on the side. A small screen opened. A green light came on. The battery was not dead.

Tom pressed play.

The screen showed a room. Tom looked at the screen. Then he looked around the bedroom. It was the same room. The same window. The same floor.

On the screen, three people stood in the room. A man, a woman, and a young boy. The man walked to the middle of the floor. He bent down and pulled up a floorboard. He put a small metal box beneath it. Then he put the floorboard back down and pressed it flat with both hands.

The woman held the boy close. They both watched the man. The woman's eyes moved to the door. Then back. Then to the door again. The boy did not move. He did not make a sound.

They looked afraid.

The tape ended. The screen went black.

Tom stood still. He looked at the floor under his feet.

He walked to the middle of the room and went down on one knee. Most of the boards were old and dark. But one board looked different. The color was a little lighter. Tom pressed it with his hand. It moved. It was loose.

He put his fingers under the edge.

"Tom!"

Mrs. Cole's voice came up from downstairs. Tom let go. He stood up fast.

"Are you up there?" she called.

Tom put the camera on the windowsill. He looked at the loose board one more time. Then he went to the door.

Mrs. Cole stood at the bottom of the stairs. She had her keys in her hand.

"I came early," she said. "Is the room ready?"

"Almost," Tom said.

She nodded. "Come down. I need to check the other rooms."

Tom followed her. She walked through the lower rooms. She opened windows and checked the doors. Tom answered her questions. Then she said, "Good work. You can go."

Tom gave her the house key. She locked the front door behind him.

He stopped on the path outside. He looked up at the bedroom window. The camera was still on the windowsill. The loose board was still closed. Something was beneath it. The family on the tape had put it there. And their faces had not looked calm.
