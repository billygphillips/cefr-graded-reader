Luca put the camera in his bag. He put the key in his pocket. Outside his flat, the street was quiet.

He walked to the Cole House.

The house looked the same as before. Dark stone, paint peeling at the windows, dead grass in the garden. Luca unlocked the front door and pushed it open. The air inside was cold and still.

He took the camera out of his bag. He did not play the tape again. He already knew the room on the tape: pale walls, a window on the left, dark wooden floors. He only needed to find it.

He began his search at the front of the house. The first room had yellow walls. Wrong. The second room had white walls, but the window was on the right. Wrong again. He moved deeper into the house. He searched each room in turn, and each room was not the one.

Then he reached the back.

The door was stiff. Luca pushed it open with his shoulder. The room was small. The walls were pale, almost grey in the flat morning light. There was a window. It was on the left.

Luca looked down. Dark wooden floorboards ran across the floor.

He put the camera on the windowsill. His search was over.

He got down on his knees. He pressed each floorboard with both hands, working slowly from the door toward the far wall. Most of them were solid and did not move. Then his hand found one near the far wall. It shifted under his palm. He pressed it again. Yes. It was loose.

He got his fingers under the edge and pulled. The board came up with a dry crack. Under it was a dark gap in the floor. Cold air rose from below.

He reached in.

His fingers found something hard. He pulled it out.

It was a metal box. Small and heavy, cold from years in the dark. He tried the lid.

The lid did not open. The box was locked.

He ran his thumb along the side of the box. He felt something uneven in the metal. He turned the box to the window light. There were letters scratched into the side. He read them slowly.

COLE.

He sat back on his heels. He looked at the scratched letters again. The name meant nothing to him. But the box was real. It was still here. Someone hid it and never came back.

His phone rang.

The sound was loud in the empty room. Luca looked at the screen. Ray Brennan.

He pressed answer.

"Ray."

"Luca." Ray's voice was calm. Too calm. "I wanted to ask. Did you find anything of value in the house?"

Luca looked at the box in his hand. The locked lid. The scratched letters.

"No," he said. "There's nothing."

A short silence.

"Good," Ray said. "Thank you."

The call ended.

Luca stayed on his knees for a moment. Then he stood up. He put the box in his bag, next to the camera. He put the loose board back in place. He walked out of the room, out of the house, and locked the door behind him.

He sat at his table. The box sat in front of him. Cold and heavy. The word COLE was scratched into the side, and the lid was locked.

Ray asked about value on the same morning Luca found the box. Not yesterday. Not the day before. Today.

That was not a coincidence.
