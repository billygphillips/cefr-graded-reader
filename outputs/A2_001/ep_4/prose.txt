The morning light came through the window of the flat. Luca sat at the empty table and turned the note over in his hand.

Two words. E. Ward.

Someone came into the flat and took the box. They moved quickly and left nothing behind — except this. He pressed the note flat on the table and stared at it. Then he folded it, put it in his jacket pocket, and stood up.

The drawer near the door was still open. He did not close it. He walked out.

The library opened at nine. Luca arrived early and waited outside on the stone step. The street was quiet. A cat walked across the road and disappeared under a fence. The librarian unlocked the door. Luca went straight to her desk.

"I need your help," he said. "I am looking for a person from Harden. The name is E. Ward."

The librarian sat down and typed the name into her computer. She looked at the screen. Her eyebrows moved up a little.

"Edward Ward," she said. "He passed away two years ago."

Luca stood still. He looked at the desk for a moment.

"Is there a family?" he asked.

She looked at the screen again. "His wife is still in town. Agnes Ward." She picked up a pen and wrote the address on a small piece of paper. She slid it across the desk. "Birch Street. Number eleven."

Luca took the address and thanked her. He walked out into the cool morning air.

Birch Street was ten minutes away
