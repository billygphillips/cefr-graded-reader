The house sat at the end of a narrow road. The paint was grey and old. The garden was tall with weeds. Luca put his work bag down and looked at the front door. It was just past eight on Saturday morning. The street was quiet.

Ray Brennan came along the path. He was a big man in a dark coat. He did not smile.

"Here." Ray held out a key. "Everything in this house goes in bags. Throw it all away."

Luca took the key. "Who lived here?" he asked.

"An old family." Ray put his hands in his pockets. "Bag everything. Don't keep anything."

Luca opened his mouth. Ray spoke first.

"Don't keep anything," Ray said again. His voice was flat and clear.

He turned and walked back to his car. He did not look back. The car started and drove away fast. Luca stood at the door. He had a key and no answers.

He put the key in the lock and went inside.

The house was cold. The air smelled of dust and old wood. Luca walked from room to room. Each room was near-empty. A broken chair here. A few old boxes there. Dusty curtains on every window.

He started in the back room. He lifted boxes and put them by the door. He dragged the broken chair into the hall. Dust rose from the floor at every step.

In the corner, a pile of dusty curtains sat on the floor. Luca lifted the top curtain. His hand touched something hard and smooth under it. He pulled it free. It was a small video camera, black and old. He turned it over. On the side was a small door. He opened the door. A tape was still inside.

He pressed the power button. Nothing. He pressed it again. Nothing. The battery was dead.

Luca looked at the camera. The tape was inside it. He wanted to know what was on the tape.

He put the camera in his work bag.

He worked for the rest of the afternoon. He filled six large bags. At five o'clock he locked the house and walked to his car. The camera was in his bag.

His flat was small and warm. Luca set the camera on the table and found a box of old cables on the shelf. He looked at them one by one. He found a cable. It fitted the small hole in the side of the camera. He put the other end in the wall.

The battery needed one hour.

Luca waited. He made tea. He looked at the camera on the table.

At last he picked it up and pressed the power button. A small light came on. He pressed play.

The tape was short. It showed a room. Dark wooden floors. Pale walls. A man and a woman stood inside. The man carried a metal box. He set it on the floor. He pressed his foot on one of the floorboards. The floorboard was loose. He lifted it with his fingers and pushed the metal box underneath. Then he stamped the loose board flat with his boot.

The woman stood near the door. She watched the man. She looked afraid.

The tape ended.

Luca sat still. He looked at the floors in the tape. Dark wood. Pale walls. He knew those floors. He knew those walls.

He reached across the table and picked up the house key.
