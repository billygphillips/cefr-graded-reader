Birch Street was quiet. Old trees lined the pavement. The houses were small and neat, with painted doors and tidy gardens. Number eleven had a green door. A pot of dead flowers sat beside it.

Luca knocked.

The door opened. A woman stood in the gap. She was old, with white hair and pale eyes. She looked at Luca. Something moved in her expression. It was quick, but it was there.

"Yes?" she said.

"My name is Luca Marr," he said. "I worked at the Cole House."

Her hand moved to the edge of the door. She began to push it closed.

"Wait." Luca lifted the video camera. He held it toward her. "I found this inside the house."

Agnes Ward stopped. She stared at the camera. The colour left her face.

For a long moment, she stood still. Then she stepped back from the door. She let him in without a word.

The room was small and warm. A fire burned low in the corner. Photographs covered one wall. An older man appeared in many of them. He had a round face and grey hair. He smiled in every picture.

Luca sat at the table. He opened the small screen on the camera and pressed play.

Agnes sat across from him. She watched the screen. Her hands lay flat on her knees.

On the tape, a man and a woman moved through a dark room. They carried the metal box between them. They put it under the floor. The man looked up. His face was clear in the light.

Agnes pressed her fingers together hard.

"That is Edward," she said. "My husband."

She looked down at her hands.

"He was dying, two years ago. He needed to confess. He told me everything."

She stopped. The room was very quiet.

"He borrowed a large sum of money from the Cole family," she said. "He signed a document. A promise to repay."

"But he did not repay it," Luca said.

"No." Agnes looked at the window. "He was afraid. He used his connections. He forced the Cole family to leave Harden. He thought the debt would go with them. And it did. For fifty years."

"And the box?" Luca said.

"The box had the document inside. And part of the original money. The document was proof. It had Edward's name on it. He was afraid to destroy it. But he was also afraid to keep it here."

Her hands began to tremble. She looked at the photographs on the wall.

"He forced out a whole family," she said quietly. "For a debt."

"What about Ray Brennan?" Luca said.

Agnes turned back to him. Her eyes were tired.

"Ray was Edward's closest friend," she said. "He knew about the debt. He knew about the document. He knew everything."

She put her hands flat on the table.

"Two days ago, Ray came to my door. He said someone found the box. He said it was not a problem."

Luca was quiet. He thought about the open drawer in his flat. The empty space under the floor.

"Ray took it," Luca said.

"I think so," Agnes said. "He wants to destroy the document. Then there is no proof. And it is finished."

She stood up. She walked to a small desk by the door. She wrote something on a piece of paper and held it out.

"Ray has an office on Cale Street," she said. "He is always there in the morning."

Luca took the paper.

Agnes stayed by the desk. She did not move. She looked very tired.

"I must confess something too," she said. "I knew. Edward told me. And I said nothing for two years."

She looked at Luca.

"I am tired of carrying this."

Luca walked out of the house. The morning air was cold on his face. He looked at the paper in his hand.

Cale Street.

The document was inside the box. The money was inside the box too. They were the only proof of what Edward did. Of what Ray knew.

And Ray had the box.

Luca began to walk fast.
