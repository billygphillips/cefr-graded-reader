The metal box sat on the table. Luca looked at the name scratched into its side. COLE. He went to the kitchen and got a small knife. He put the blade into the lock and pushed. The metal did not move. He tried harder. The knife slipped and cut the wood of the table. He put it down.

The box was not going to open that way.

He needed more. He needed to know who the Cole family were and why they left the house. He could not find that in his flat. He left the box on the table. He put on his jacket and walked to the library.

The public library was at the far end of Harden's main street. It was cool inside and quiet. It smelled of old paper. Luca went to the desk and asked the librarian for old town newspapers. The woman brought him a large box. It was full of copies from the 1970s. He carried it to a table near the window and sat down.

He looked at the newspapers one by one. Most of them told him nothing. Then he found a newspaper from July 1974. Near the back, there was a short article. The headline read: COLE FAMILY LEAVES HARDEN. He read the article slowly. The Cole family left the town without warning. They gave no reason. No one in Harden knew where they went. Their house passed to a new owner a short time later.

The article said nothing more.

Luca returned to the desk. He asked the librarian for property records from the same year. She brought him a thin brown folder. He opened it and found a record of the house sale. He read through each line. Most of the names in the record meant nothing to him. But near the bottom of the page, next to the word "witness", there was a name: E. Ward. Luca looked at it for a long moment. He took a piece of paper and wrote two things: Cole family — left Harden, July 1974. And: E. Ward.

He left the library. The afternoon was grey. The street was quiet. He walked back towards his flat.

At his door, he stopped.

The door was not fully closed. It was open a small amount. He locked it before he left. He was sure of that.

He pushed the door open slowly. The flat was still. He walked to the table.

The metal box was not there.

He looked at the drawer beside his bed. It was open. He walked to the drawer and looked inside. The things inside were not in the right place. Someone moved them. He checked under the table. He checked behind the door. He looked on the floor near the window.

The box was stolen.

Luca stood at the table and stared at the empty space. He looked at the open drawer. He thought about the stolen box and the name on its side. He thought about who opened that drawer. He thought about who opened the door. One person knew Luca had the box. That person was Ray Brennan.
